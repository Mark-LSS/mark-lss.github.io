This module covers Daughter Craft diving operations: safety requirements, operational constraints, emergency responses and diving procedures. All personnel must complete this familiarisation before participating in Daughter Craft operations.

01
Purpose & Scope
What this manual covers and why it exists

This familiarisation manual covers the operational requirements of the N-Sea Daughter Craft and Support Craft as used for offshore diving operations. It is based on BMS-OP-0630-MA-001 Rev 2.9 and is intended as a training tool for all personnel who work on or in support of Daughter Craft operations.

The Daughter Craft is a dedicated small vessel used for performing diving operations in locations not accessible from the Mother Vessel. All diving is conducted under surface supply (umbilical) methodology and is restricted to the no-decompression range. No planned in-water decompression is permitted.

ℹ
Scope

Safety requirements — System overview — Personnel roles — Pre-dive planning — Launch & recovery — Mooring — Diving constraints — Nitrox — Gas management — Emergency responses. Must be read alongside BMS-OP-0600-PR-001 and BMS-OP-0630-PR-101.

02
Safety Requirements
HIRA, MOC, PTW and DP Status requirements

All operations must be planned in accordance with the Hazard Identification Risk Assessment (HIRA) and Task Based Risk Assessment (TBRA). These must be reviewed on site before commencement and all amendments documented.

📋 HIRA & TBRA

A HIRA is required prior to mobilisation. A Level 2 TBRA must be completed on site before operations begin. The HIRA must address casualty transfer routes to the DDC or medical facility.

🔄 Management of Change

If procedures require modification during the operation, the MOC process (BMS-SH-0250-PR-001) must be followed. An MOC generates a form and, if required, a new TBRA.

📄 Permit to Work

The Daughter Craft may only be launched under a valid PTW. Within the 500m zone of an installation, a PTW and isolation certificates are required. Appendix A (BMS-AM-0450-FO-137) must be completed.

🔻 Mother Vessel DP Status

The Mother Vessel must be on DP with a green status light prior to and during all launch and recovery operations. DP comms links must be tested before launch.

⚠
Position of Safety

No personnel are to be positioned where they could be struck by a moving load or caught between the Daughter Craft and Mother Vessel hull during launch, recovery or alongside operations.

Pre-Dive Supervisor Confirmation (Selected Items)

  • Weather forecast suitable for the anticipated dive duration
  • PTW obtained and valid — isolation certificates in place
  • DDC available for immediate use — DDC operator carrying radio
  • All checklists completed and logged in Vessel Log and Operations Log
  • Mooring plan approved by OIM and in place
  • Dive Plan completed and briefed to all team
  • Mother Vessel DP green status confirmed
  • All on-shift personnel declared fitness to dive / fitness for duty — recorded in Operations Log (HSE L103 ACOP)
  • Pre-dive hardwire comms test: Working Diver, Standby Diver and Supervisor confirmed — logged
  • Standby Diver at minimum dressing standard at water entry point before Working Diver enters

Dive System & Vessels
Daughter Craft, Support Craft and LARS overview

N-Sea Daughter Crafts are typically MCA Category 1 or 2 sea-going vessels, nominally 10–15m in length, diesel-powered with twin engines. Each Daughter Craft LARS is a vessel-specific, stand-alone system that can be installed on any suitable Mother Vessel.

⚓ Vessel (Typical)
  • MCA Category 1 or 2 certification
  • 10–15m length, diesel twin-engine
  • Purpose-built for surface-supply diving
  • Umbilical management station and gas supply fitted
  • Buoyancy compartments for residual buoyancy
  • Minimum 4 fire extinguishers + CO2 engine room system
  • MCA Cat B first aid kit minimum (offshore diving)

04
Roles & Responsibilities
Key personnel duties on the Daughter Craft
👷 Diving Supervisor — Key Duties▼

Person in charge of all Daughter Craft diving operations. Key duties:

  • Overall safety of all diving operations
  • Confirming all checklists are complete before any dive
  • Confirming fitness declarations for all on-shift personnel (HSE L103 ACOP)
  • Confirming Standby Diver is at minimum readiness before Working Diver enters
  • Pre-dive hardwire comms test — Working Diver, Standby Diver and Supervisor confirmed
  • Confirming DDC is available and DDC operator has radio
  • Maintaining Operations Log with all mandatory items including NDL applied and any adjustments
  • Notifying Mother Vessel immediately of any decompression obligation
🤦 Standby Diver — Minimum Readiness Standard▼

The Standby Diver must be at the following minimum dressing standard before the Working Diver enters the water, and must remain so until the Working Diver is fully recovered to deck:

  1. Fully dressed in diving suit and personal equipment
  2. Umbilical connected, pressurised and communications tested
  3. Bail-out bottle charged and worn on the diver
  4. Helmet on with visor open
  5. Positioned at water entry point

This standard must be documented in the Dive Plan and confirmed by the Supervisor before Working Diver entry.

⚓ Coxswain — Key Duties▼

Responsible for the Daughter Craft and all personnel on board from embarkation to disembarkation. Key duties:

  • Complete vessel equipment checklists — record in Vessel Log
  • Confirm DP green status before launch and recovery
  • Confirm all conditions are suitable before instructing Davit Operator to launch
  • Maintain radio contact with Mother Vessel bridge at all times
  • Diving shall not be undertaken when the vessel is beam-on to wind/tide unless station can be maintained
  • Operations shall not be conducted upwind/up-tide of a structural hazard
🔧 Dive Technician — Key Duties▼
  • Complete Dive Technician Daughter Craft Dive System Daily Checklist — record in Vessel Log
  • Maintain LOLER Register for all lifting equipment, slings and accessories
  • Manage gas supply, gas analysis records and cylinder labelling
  • Pre-dive umbilical inspection and pull test (5 checks) — record in Operations Log
  • Planned maintenance schedule compliance per N-Sea PMS

⚠
100m Proximity — Diver Deployed

The Mother Vessel must not come within 100 metres of the working diver's deployment zone AND must not come within 4 × the maximum umbilical length of the Daughter Craft. Both constraints apply concurrently.

08
Weather & Operating Limits
Sea state, visibility and SIMOPS constraints
🌊 Sea State Limit

Maximum 2m significant wave height. Local conditions, swell period and seasickness may reduce this further. The Dive Supervisor and Coxswain jointly make the call.

👁 Visibility

Clear visual contact between Daughter Craft and Mother Vessel bridge must be maintained at all times. If not possible, a nominated observer with direct radio contact to the bridge must be designated. AODC 034 applies for restricted visibility diving.

🔴 SIMOPS

No unplanned third-party vessel SIMOPS in the vicinity during diving. Any planned SIMOPS must be covered by the HIRA. If unplanned SIMOPS arise, diving halts until the area is clear.

⛈
Weather Decision Authority

Any decision to commence, continue or cease operations in deteriorating weather rests with the Dive Supervisor or Coxswain. Both have statutory duties. Neither can be overridden by the OPM or Client on safety grounds. A Toolbox Talk must be held when conditions are marginal.

09
Launch & Recovery (LOLER)
Lift Plan, LOLER compliance and crew boarding

Launch and recovery is a man-riding lifting operation regulated by LOLER 1998. A Lift Plan is mandatory and must be tested prior to first deployment. A trial must be conducted after leaving harbour and before entering the 500m zone.

Lift Plan — Minimum Considerations

  • Crew embarkation and disembarkation points clearly identified
  • Height of lift and load stability assessed
  • Effect of sea state, wind and hull slap on the lifting operation
  • Belay points to control swing during launch and recovery
  • Tag line on main hook to control pendulum
  • Crane angle optimised to reduce pendulum effect
  • Banksman in radio contact with Crane Operator at all times
  • Davit Operator competence confirmed — on-board training course completed
  • Jib position when tendered alongside identified
ℹ
LOLER Form F8

Form F8 (6-monthly LARS examination certificate from a competent person) must be on board before any operations commence. LOLER Register must be maintained by the Dive Technician and available for inspection at all times.

Equipment specifications and safe mooring practices

Mooring points must be approved by the Offshore Installation Manager (OIM). The exact location of live risers, outfalls and prohibited zones must be clarified by the client before mooring points are used.

⚓
Chafe Protection

Mooring plans must show how sufficient spring is provided to prevent snatch loads. Anti-chafe measures must be in place at all contact points. Breaking strains must be known.

11
Communications
Mother Vessel, platform and pre-deployment protocol
📡 Mother Vessel

Daughter Craft must maintain radio communications with the Mother Vessel bridge at all times. If communications are lost, diving operations shall halt immediately until reliably re-established.

🏮 Platform / Installation

When working alongside a platform, comms must be established before operations begin. The platform must be kept informed of all location changes. Loss of comms = halt operations. Correct vessel terminology (Daughter Craft vs Support Craft) is mandatory on all radio traffic.

Pre-Deployment Communication Protocol

  1. 1
    DP Status Confirmation

    Confirm Mother Vessel DP is green. DP officer informed of pending launch.

  2. 2
    Channel Assignment

    VHF/UHF working channels and emergency channels confirmed and logged.

  3. 3
    Hardwire Diver Comms Test

    Pre-dive hardwire test: Working Diver → Standby Diver → Supervisor. Primary and back-up systems confirmed. Channel recorded in Operations Log.

  4. 4
    DDC Operator Confirmation

    DDC-qualified operator on Mother Vessel confirmed with radio. Ready to receive.

  5. 5
    Platform Informed

    Installation informed of commencement of diving and plant shutdowns confirmed via PTW.

Diving Constraints & NDL
No-decompression limits, dry suit adjustment and decompression overrun procedure
⛔
No Planned Decompression

There is no decompression facility on the Daughter Craft. All dives are restricted to the no-decompression range. Planned in-water decompression and surface decompression are not permitted. The DDC on the Mother Vessel is for emergency use only.

Operational Constraints

ConstraintLimit / RuleAuthority
Gas usage ruleWorking diver gas use shall not exceed 50% (half) of total gas volume at diver entry. Deviation requires TBRA and DS sign-off.N-Sea deviation from IMCA D040 one-third rule. More conservative due to cold water, long umbilicals, no on-board DDC, Standby Diver reserve.
Dry suit NDL reduction10% reduction to all NDLs when dry suits are used instead of hot water suits. Applied to air NDL and Nitrox EAD-derived NDLs.BMS-OP-0600-PR-001 (Sterk tables)
Mother Vessel proximityNot within 4 × maximum umbilical length of Daughter Craft; not within 100m of diver deployment zoneMA-001 §7.4
DDC transfer timeMaximum 15 minutes working depth to blow-down. 20 minutes for training only.MA-001 §7.9
Umbilical lengthMaximum 75 metres standard. MOD and proximity calculated from actual length.IMCA D040

Decompression Obligation Overrun Procedure (§7.2.2)

  1. 1
    Immediate Mother Vessel Notification

    Notify Mother Vessel immediately with depth, bottom time and decompression obligation details.

  2. 2
    Gas Sufficiency Check

    Check remaining gas. Minimum 50 bar reserve required before committing to in-water stop. If insufficient, proceed to Step 5.

  3. 3
    Table Selection

    Select BMS-OP-0600-PR-001 LND15 overrun procedure (Section 8.3.2). For Nitrox dives, use Safety EAD to select equivalent air table.

  4. 4
    Standby Diver

    Maintain Standby Diver at deployment station throughout the in-water stop.

  5. 5
    DDC Entry on Surface

    On surfacing, initiate DDC entry immediately. DDC operator alerted at Step 1.

  6. 6
    Incident Report

    Mandatory incident report. OPM and Master formally notified.

Nitrox & Gas Management
PO2 limits, MOD calculation, NDL adjustments and gas analysis
ConditionMax PO2Authority
Working limit1.4 barIMCA D048
Contingency / short excursion1.6 barIMCA D048 — not to be used as working limit
ℹ
O2 Toxicity

Daily OTU tracking must be performed per the Sterk tables. All OTU values must be recorded in the Operations Log. If O2 toxicity is suspected underwater, switch main supply to air immediately.

MOD Formula

Maximum Operating Depth at working PO2 limit of 1.4 bar:

MOD (msw) = ( PO2_limit / FO2 − 1 ) × 10

Worked Example — EAN32

FO2 = 0.32, PO2 limit = 1.4 bar

MOD = (1.4/0.32 − 1) × 10 = 33.75 → 33m

Always round down. Never round up.

⚠
Dive Plan Requirement

Both adjustments must be explicitly stated in the Dive Plan with the calculation shown. The Diving Supervisor must sign off the adjusted NDL before the diver enters the water.

Mandatory Gas Analysis Requirements

  • Analysis required for every cylinder before use
  • Cylinder label must show: O2%, MOD and date of analysis
  • Analyser calibration must be recorded in Gas Analysis Record
  • Dive Supervisor must verify analysis record before approving dive
  • Cylinders not conforming to analysis requirements must not be used
ℹ
Oxygen Service (IMCA D031)

All equipment used with Nitrox mixes containing more than 25% O2 must be oxygen-clean to IMCA D031 standard. This includes hoses, valves, regulators and cylinders used in the transfer of such mixes.

15
Emergency Responses
Click each scenario to reveal the response protocol

All personnel must be familiar with these emergency response procedures before participating in Daughter Craft operations. These correspond to the drills in BMS-OP-0600-DTM-002 (Appendix C).

📡Emergency Signals

Emergency signals are issued by verbal instruction from the person in charge. The Mother Vessel is notified by VHF/UHF per project communication protocols.

🚣Collision
Daughter Craft Response
  • Inform Support/Rescue Craft
  • Transfer to Support Craft if required (not alongside until instructed)
  • Return to Mother Vessel and recover inboard
Diver Response
  • Leave jobsite, make way to swim line
  • Prepare for recovery to surface
⚓Grounding
  • Inform Support/Rescue Craft
  • Assess situation — tow-off or float-off if safe
  • Assess damage before proceeding
  • Transfer to Support Craft if required
  • Return to Mother Vessel or transit to safe haven with attendant vessel
🔏Davit Failure
  • Await power restoration (vessel blackout)
  • Deck Foreman to liaise with bridge re: alternative power supply or spreader beam crane recovery
  • If recovery not possible: tow, tie alongside or transit to safe haven with Mother Vessel

Note: Aberlour & Edradour has bespoke Davit with emergency HPO.

⛈Weather Deterioration
  • Consider going alongside installation standby vessel
  • Secure Daughter Craft alongside or tow per Master direction
  • Use available lee to best advantage
  • Transit to pre-planned safe haven if near shore

Safe havens must be identified during project planning as part of HIRA mitigation measures.

🔪Engine Failure
  • Check fuel supply
  • Inform Support/Rescue Craft
  • Reinstate fuel — restart engine (vessel is twin engine)
  • Operations cannot continue unless both engines are working
  • Dual engine failure: deploy anchor, seek third-party assistance
🔥Fire On Board
Daughter Craft Response
  • Fight fire if possible with suitable extinguisher
  • Inform Mother Vessel, platform, standby vessel, Support/Rescue Craft
  • Instruct Diver to return to surface (DS to give special instructions if ladder unsafe)
  • Request assistance from installation firefighting
  • Transfer to Support Craft if required
Diver Response
  • Leave jobsite, go to swim line
  • Prepare for surface recovery as directed by Supervisor
🩹Loss of Moorings
Daughter Craft Response
  • Coxswain informs Support Craft and installation
  • DS instructs Diver — typically recover to surface
  • Deploy anchor if appropriate
  • Clear obstructions, start engines, relocate, re-establish moorings
Diver Response
  • Prepare for surface recovery
  • Follow DS instructions
🚨Unplanned General Alarm
  • Stop diving operations immediately
  • Recover Diver to Daughter Craft
  • Receive VHF confirmation of alarm status from platform
  • Let go moored position
  • Return and recover to Mother Vessel deck
📡Mother Vessel Must Relocate
  • Contact Mother Vessel on VHF — confirm status
  • Stop diving operations
  • Recover Diver to Daughter Craft
  • Standby for recovery to Mother Vessel
⚠High O2 Alarm
  • Secure and isolate all ignition sources immediately
  • Trace leak and secure
  • Ventilate the area
  • Wait for normal atmosphere before recommencing operations
🏉Man Overboard
  • Immediately assign crew member to keep MOB in sight — priority above all else
  • Throw lifebuoy with light stick
  • Inform Mother Vessel — Mother Vessel takes overall management
  • Request Support/Rescue Craft

Secondary drowning risk: any immersion casualty must be observed for 4–90 hours post-recovery. Hypothermic casualties: handle gently — can present as deceased but may be revivable.

🏃Injured Diver / Diving Emergency
Surface Response
  • Instruct Standby Diver to locate Working Diver immediately
  • Bad gas / O2 toxicity: switch to back-up gas (Nitrox: switch to air)
  • Warn DMT on Mother Vessel — prepare DDC
  • Request Support/Rescue Craft to standby
Standby Diver Response
  • Follow umbilical to locate Diver
  • If gas lost: open bailout
  • If unconscious: confirm back-up gas is on, open free-flow slightly
  • Recover Diver as quickly as possible; assist ventilation; ensure exhaling on ascent
  • On surface: check airway; commence mouth-to-mouth if not breathing
Transfer to Mother Vessel
  • Stabilise and place on stretcher if required
  • DS determines transfer method — Support Craft or Daughter Craft
  • Transfer at best speed to Mother Vessel for DDC entry or shore facility
💦Bilge Alarm (DTM D12)
  • Assess rate of water ingress immediately
  • Confirm bilge pump is operational
  • Notify Mother Vessel and Support Craft
  • If controllable: continue monitoring, record activation in Vessel Log
  • If uncontrollable: initiate Section 8.17 (Floundering & Flooding) immediately
🚴Floundering & Flooding (DTM D13)
  • Immediate VHF MAYDAY / emergency notification to Mother Vessel, installation, all vessels
  • Emergency diver ascent if necessary — DS to instruct
  • All personnel to life jackets immediately
  • Support/Rescue Craft alongside without waiting for instruction
  • Personnel transfer: injured first, then Diver, then remaining crew
  • Activate EPIRB/PLB
  • Notify maritime authority

N-Sea Daughter Crafts are designed with buoyancy compartments. Manage evacuation in a controlled manner unless imminent danger of capsize.

16
Do's & Don'ts
Critical behavioural rules for Daughter Craft operations
✓ Always Do
  • Confirm Mother Vessel DP is green before any launch or recovery
  • Complete all checklists and record them in the Vessel Log and Ops Log before any operation
  • Ensure Standby Diver is at full readiness standard before Working Diver enters the water
  • Perform pre-dive hardwire comms test — Working Diver, Standby Diver and Supervisor
  • Apply the 50% gas rule — stop at half the total gas volume available at dive entry
  • Reduce NDL by 10% when dry suits are used; apply EAD for Nitrox; combine both adjustments where applicable
  • Ensure LOLER Form F8 is on board and current before LARS operations
  • Record fitness declarations for all on-shift personnel in the Operations Log
  • Halt diving immediately and recover Diver on any comms failure or general alarm
  • Notify Mother Vessel immediately if any decompression obligation arises
✕ Never Do
  • Conduct planned decompression from the Daughter Craft — there is no DDC on board
  • Launch without a valid PTW and OIM-approved mooring plan
  • Conduct diving with the vessel beam-on to wind or tide unless station can be maintained
  • Allow the Working Diver to enter the water if the Standby Diver is not at minimum readiness standard
  • Use Nitrox equipment not oxygen-cleaned (IMCA D031) for mixes over 25% O2
  • Exceed 1.4 bar PO2 during normal working dives
  • Allow the Mother Vessel within 4 × umbilical length of the Daughter Craft or 100m of the diver during diving
  • Allow the Support Craft alongside the Daughter Craft without instruction from the DS/Coxswain
  • Continue operations in sea states above 2m without a fresh risk assessment
  • Terminate a man overboard search while there is any reasonable hope of rescue

Q & A Review
Click each question to reveal the answer before attempting the assessment
Why is the 50% gas rule used instead of the IMCA D040 one-third rule?▼

The N-Sea 50% rule is a more conservative deviation from the IMCA D040 one-third rule. It is applied specifically to Daughter Craft operations because of: extended umbilical lengths in cold water; the absence of an on-board recompression chamber; the need to retain margin for the Standby Diver's gas; contingency decompression if an overrun occurs; and bailout reserves. The rationale must be recorded in the Dive Plan.

What is the combined NDL adjustment for a dry suit Nitrox dive?▼

Both adjustments are applied concurrently. First, calculate the Safety EAD from the Sterk tables (BMS-OP-0600-PR-001 §11.14) to derive the equivalent air NDL. Then apply a further 10% reduction for dry suit use. Both adjustments must be stated in the Dive Plan and signed off by the Diving Supervisor.

What happens if diving communications fail underwater?▼

Diving operations shall halt immediately. The Standby Diver may be deployed to locate the Working Diver. Operations do not resume until communications are reliably re-established on both primary and back-up systems. The failure and resolution must be recorded in the Operations Log.

What does the Standby Diver do if the Working Diver is unconscious underwater?▼

The Standby Diver follows the Working Diver's umbilical to locate them. If gas supply is lost, opens the bailout. If gas is intact, opens free-flow slightly. Confirms with Supervisor that main supply is isolated and back-up is on. Recovers Diver as quickly as possible; assists ventilation; ensures Diver is exhaling on ascent. On surface: checks airway; begins mouth-to-mouth if not breathing.

What is the minimum O2 supply required on the Mother Vessel?▼

90m³ minimum at the start of diving operations. This is one of the 11 minimum qualification criteria for Mother Vessel selection (Section 05). Any departure requires an MOC and must be documented in the project workpack.

What should you do if the Bilge Alarm activates and water ingress is uncontrollable?▼

Initiate the Floundering and Flooding procedure (Section 8.17) immediately. This includes: MAYDAY notification, emergency diver ascent instruction, life jackets on all personnel, Support Craft alongside, personnel transfer, EPIRB/PLB activation and maritime authority notification. Do not delay.

What is the maximum PO2 for Nitrox diving and what if O2 toxicity is suspected?▼

Working limit is 1.4 bar PO2 (IMCA D048). 1.6 bar is a short-excursion contingency limit only. If O2 toxicity is suspected underwater, switch the main supply to air immediately. Notify Supervisor; deploy Standby Diver if required.

Why must the Mother Vessel DP be green before launch and recovery?▼

Launch and recovery is a man-riding lifting operation. An unexpected movement of the Mother Vessel during launch or recovery could result in fatal crushing or swinging of the Daughter Craft. DP green status confirms the vessel is maintaining position within acceptable parameters. DP personnel must be informed of all pre-dive Toolbox Talks and communication links must be tested before launch.
