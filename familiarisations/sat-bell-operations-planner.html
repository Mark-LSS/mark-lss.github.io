○ Single Bell — 3 Teams · 8 hr Bell Run · 3 Runs/Day 3×8 = 24 hrs · Stable · No ratchet

Three teams on staggered 12-hour shifts, each with an 8-hour bell run. Three runs of 8 hours fills the 24-hour cycle exactly — no team is skipped and no drift occurs. Ideally the work period falls in the middle of the 12-hour shift. If diving cannot proceed for any reason, teams remain in their eating and sleeping patterns until diving recommences. The team at the beginning of its shift will be the one to restart operations.

Staggered lockout technique: To extend the working window without breaching individual lockout limits, Diver 1 exits the bell first. One hour later, Diver 2 locks out. When Diver 1 completes his 6-hour lockout he returns to the bell — Diver 2 continues working alone for one further hour, then also returns. The bell is then recovered. This produces a 7-hour in-water window from a 6-hour individual limit. However the two solo hours (one at each end of the lockout window) carry reduced productivity — only one diver is on the job. ● Twin Bell — 4 Teams · On-Bottom Turnaround · 4 Runs/Day No team skipped · 48 diver-hrs/day · +33%

Four teams on the same 12-hour shift pattern. With a single bell, four teams × 8-hour runs = 32 hours — only 3 runs fit in a 24-hour day, so one team must be skipped each day and divers ratchet forward. Twin bell eliminates this entirely: team 1 (Bell 1) is on the worksite while team 2 (Bell 2) is being deployed to seabed. The on-bottom turnaround maximises time at the worksite and achieves all 4 runs in 24 hours. No team is ever required to skip a dive.

Saturation Shift Patterns — Operational Policy & Criteria
General Criteria
‣ The shift plan must provide a minimum 12-hour rest period between work periods.
‣ The 12-hour rest does not include diving or pre/post-dive checks.
‣ For 3-man operations, no diver shall take part in more than one 6-hour lockout within a pre-planned 24-hour period.
‣ For 2-man operations, no diver shall take part in more than one 4-hour lockout within a pre-planned 24-hour period.
‣ No ratcheting of the whole dive team — i.e. cycling the entire team in less than 24 hours in order to gain work time.
‣ On completion of the first dive during a saturation period, the dive team will be given their shift plan. Ideally the work period will be in the middle of the 12-hour shift.
‣ If the dive team are unable to dive, teams shall remain in their sleeping and eating patterns until diving recommences. It will be the team at the beginning of their shift who will recommence diving operations.
⚠ 4-Team Single Bell — Ratchet
4 teams × 8 hr runs = 32 hr cycle in a 24-hr day. Only 3 full runs fit — one team must be skipped each day.
The team to be missed is decided by the Dive Supervisor and LSS, per IMCA guidance, depending on expected dive duration and work scope.
Divers will be given notice if expected to start a dive at the beginning of their 12-hour shift.
Prolonged Operations
Over prolonged diving activities, it may be necessary to decompress some divers to bring in replacement teams. As this can take several days, a four-team rota may need to revert temporarily to three teams. Teams must still have a 12-hour rest period. The Dive Supervisor and LSS agree the new pattern to minimise disruption.
● Twin Bell Philosophy
General shift patterns remain as above. With two bells, added flexibility and time savings are permitted as team rotation may be undertaken using "on-bottom turnarounds".
This is where Team 1 (Bell 1) may be at the worksite whilst Team 2 (Bell 2) is being deployed to seabed — maximising diver time at the worksite.
During twin bell operations there will be no requirement for teams to skip a dive as is often found when diving on a single bell system.

Decompression Profile: Dynamic Hold Cycle (Project Standard).
Project generation uses the first visible starting teams as the base slots. Each replacement team blows down when the previous team in that slot reaches its pre-deco hold start.
Print / Save PDF opens a clean landscape print report. Export to Excel creates a formatted workbook with Project, Compliance, Teams and Timeline sheets. Prepare Email opens your default email client with a summary.

How to Fill In This Planner

1. Project Setup
Enter Project Name, Project Number, Vessel, Project Start, Project Duration, Max Sat Days, and Initial Teams Blown In Together.
2. Starting Teams
Use Set Initial Team Count first. This creates the starting teams blown in together at the beginning of the project.
3. Team Composition
Each team is a 3-man bell team: Bellman · Diver 1 · Diver 2. Names are entered per team card below.
4. Team Inputs
For each team: enter Team Label, Storage Depth, Blowdown Date/Time, Required Surface Date/Time. Then enter diver names.
5. Surface Date
Required Surface is the target time by which that team must be back on surface. Planner works back to calculate pre-deco hold start and decompression start.
6. Rotation Generation
When starting teams are complete, press Generate Teams for Project. New teams are generated in waves using starting teams as rotation slots.
7. What the Tool Checks
Tracks time in saturation, time left before hold, time left before surface, and whether required surface time is inside the maximum saturation limit.
8. Notes
Bell run and in-water limits are not entered here. This tool is for saturation exposure, decompression timing, and rotation planning. Use Bell Rotation tab for bell efficiency.
